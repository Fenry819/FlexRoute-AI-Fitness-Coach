Hypertrophy and RIR (Reps in Reserve): To maximize muscle growth, you must train close to mechanical failure. The sweet spot for hypertrophy is 1 to 3 RIR. If you stop a set and could have easily done 5 more reps, you are just resting, not training. You must push the muscle to the point where the rep speed physically slows down.

Systemic Fatigue and Junk Volume: Junk volume kills your gains. If your workouts last 2.5 hours and you are doing 25 sets per body part, your body is spending all its energy recovering from systemic fatigue rather than synthesizing new muscle tissue. Cap your workouts at 6 to 10 hard, high-quality sets per muscle group per session. 

Diet and Macros for Bulking: To gain muscle optimally without getting fat, you need a slight caloric surplus of about 250 to 500 calories above maintenance. Consume 0.8 to 1 gram of protein per pound of body weight. Do not cut carbohydrates; carbs are the primary fuel source for high-intensity lifting and create a highly anabolic environment in the body.

Diet for Cutting and Fat Loss: To lose fat while preserving hard-earned muscle, aim for a strict daily deficit of 500 calories. Keep protein very high (up to 1.2 grams per pound of body weight) to prevent muscle catabolism. Training volume (number of sets) in the gym may need to drop slightly as your physical recovery capacity diminishes in a caloric deficit, but training intensity (the weight on the bar) must stay as high as possible to signal the body to keep the muscle.

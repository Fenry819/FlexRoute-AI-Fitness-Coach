Patellar Tendonitis (Jumper's Knee): To fix front knee pain from squatting or jumping, stop dive-bombing your squats. You need to build tendon capacity. Use slow tempo Spanish squats, backward sled pulls, or pin squats. Isometric holds (like wall sits) for 45 seconds are the gold standard for immediate tendon pain relief before lifting.

Rotator Cuff and Shoulder Impingement: If your shoulder hurts during bench press, check your scapular retraction. Rehab with strict face pulls, focusing on external rotation. Avoid heavy barbell overhead presses until pain-free; substitute with unilateral dumbbell presses or landmine presses to allow for natural scapular upward rotation without grinding the joint.

Lower Back Pain (Tweaks and Bulges): If you tweaked your back deadlifting or squatting, stop all loaded spinal flexion immediately. Implement the "McGill Big 3" (Bird-Dog, Side Plank, Curl-up) daily to build extreme core stiffness. When returning to lifting, use kettlebell block pulls or trap bar deadlifts before pulling from the floor to limit the range of motion and protect the lumbar spine.

Elbow Pain (Tennis/Golfer's Elbow): Inside or outside elbow pain from pulling movements (pull-ups, rows) means the tendons are inflamed. Stop doing heavy weighted chin-ups. Rehab with high-rep, low-weight eccentric wrist curls. Switch to neutral grip (palms facing each other) attachments for all back exercises until the inflammation subsides.
